About Us

Pure Pound (PUP)

Pure Pound (PUP) is the cryptocurrency for emerging markets. It prides itself as the medium of exchange for the 4th Industrial Revolution and the Internet of Things. As an advocate of data mining and agriculture, it is uniquely positioned as a tool for improving lives in the fastest growing region in the world. We envision SE Asia as the next center of economic and technological development in the world.

Innovative Payment Network

Pure Pound is an innovative payment network. It is a new kind of monetary currency. Pure Pound uses peer-to-peer technology to operate with no central authority or banks; managing transactions and the issuing of purepounds is carried out collectively by the network. Pure Pound is open-source; its design is public, nobody owns or controls Pure Pound and everyone can take part. Through many of its unique properties, Pure Pound allows exciting uses that could not be covered by any previous payment system.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Pure Pound?

Pure Pound is a consensus network that enables a new payment system, which defines a completely different form of digital money. It is the a 5th generation decentralized peer-to-peer payment network that is powered by its users with no central authority or middle-men. From a user perspective, Pure Pound is pretty much like cash, gold, and silver for the Internet. Pure Pound can also be seen as the most prominent triple entry bookkeeping system in existence. PUP is a true global innovation for the emerging markets.

Who created Pure Pound?

Pure Pound is an application of the POUND Protocol. It is an implementation of a concept called "cryptocurrency", which was first described in 1998 by Wei Dai on the cypherpunks mailing list, suggesting the idea of a new form of money that uses cryptography to control its creation and transactions, rather than a central authority. It is inspired by the block-chain technology that powers the BTC network. The first Pure Pound specification and proof of concept was based in the 2009 open-source cryptography mailing list by Satoshi Nakamoto, called as the White Paper for Bitcoin. The crypto-community has since grown exponentially with many developers working on similar projects like Bitcoin, Ethereum, Ripple, Iota, and the like.

Who controls the Pure Pound network?

Nobody owns the Pure Pound network much like no one owns the technology behind e-mail. Pure Pound is controlled by all Pure Pound users around the world. While developers are improving the software, they can't force a change in the Pure Pound protocol because all users are free to choose what software and version they use. In order to stay compatible with each other, all users need to use software complying with the same rules. Pure Pound can only work correctly with a complete consensus among all users. Therefore, all users and developers have a strong incentive to protect this consensus.

How does Pure Pound work?

From a user perspective, Pure Pound is nothing more than a mobile app or computer program that provides a personal Pure Pound wallet and allows a user to send and receive pure pounds with them. This is how Pure Pound works for most users. Behind the scenes, the Pure Pound network is sharing a public ledger called the "APL". This ledger contains every transaction ever processed, allowing a user's computer to verify the validity of each transaction. The authenticity of each transaction is protected by digital signatures anchored on the Cryptographic Hash Algorithm (CHA) 1024-bit hashing technology which was developed uniquely for PUP. This process corresponds to the sending addresses, allowing all users to have full control over sending pounds from their own Pure Pound addresses. In addition, anyone can process transactions using the computing power of specialized hardware, with key focus on using mobile phones, and earn a reward in pounds for this service. This is often called "mining" or "digital minting".

Is Pure Pound really used by people?

Yes. There are a growing number of businesses and individuals using Pure Pound. This includes brick-and-mortar businesses like restaurants, apartments, and law firms, as well as popular online services. While Pure Pound remains a relatively new phenomenon, it is growing fast and an incredible rate.

How difficult is it to make a Pure Pound payment?

Pure Pound payments are easier to make than debit or credit card purchases, and can be received without a merchant account. Payments are made from a wallet application, either on your computer or smartphone, by entering the recipient's address, the payment amount, and pressing send. To make it easier to enter a recipient's address, many wallets can obtain the address by scanning a QR code or touching two phones together with NFC technology. An off-line payment system is also being developed.
